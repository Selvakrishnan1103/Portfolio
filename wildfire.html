The Fire That Scared Us


Introduction


Today was an unforgettable day as my family and I faced an unexpected fire in our garden. It was a moment of panic, struggle, and teamwork. Here’s how it all unfolded.


The Start of the Fire


While I was sleeping, my mother suddenly woke me and my brother up, calling us to the garden. We had no idea what was happening. As soon as we reached, she pointed out that a fire was spreading all around the garden and nearby areas.


The Challenges We Faced


My mother, brother, grandfather, and I rushed to see the fire expanding rapidly. The dry environment made it worse since there had been no rainfall recently. The well had dried up, and we had very little water available in a drum.

Our garden had no crops due to the drought, and all the dried plants easily caught fire. We first tried using buckets of water, but it didn’t work. The fire was approaching the engine room, which housed our engine motor. We had to act quickly to save it.


Fighting the Fire


We then noticed some gunny bags lying nearby. Luckily, we had three of them, and we tried using them to control the fire. At one point, we lost hope as the fire spread uncontrollably. We thought we had to let it burn, but then, with God’s grace, we got a new opportunity.

There was a small canal-like mounted area where the fire had stopped at one end, leaving us with only one direction to fight. We regained our determination and resumed our efforts. This time, we received help from my aunt and her sons, who joined us in stopping the fire.


Finding the Cause


Until this point, we had no idea how the fire started. Later, someone arrived and informed us that he had also been trying to stop a fire in his own garden. Eventually, he discovered that a wire had been cut from an electric pole. Due to strong winds, the sparks spread the fire across the area.

He immediately contacted the village president and the electricity commission officer. Finally, using sand and water from our old garden, we managed to extinguish all the burning areas.


Conclusion


Lessons Learned


This experience taught us the importance of being prepared for emergencies and working together in times of crisis. If not for our collective effort and quick thinking, we could have lost valuable property.


Final Thoughts

"Strength does not come from physical capacity. It comes from an indomitable will." — Mahatma Gandhi

Thank you for reading. Stay safe and always be prepared for unexpected emergencies!
